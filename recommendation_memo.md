# Executive Summary

This analysis evaluated the effectiveness of a new onboarding and activation campaign designed to improve user conversion and early engagement. Users were assigned to either a Control group (existing onboarding experience) or a Treatment group (new onboarding experience).

The results indicate that the Treatment group achieved a significantly higher Paid Conversion Rate compared to the Control group. Statistical testing confirms that the observed improvement is unlikely to be due to random variation.

Based on the experiment results and supporting business metrics, the recommendation is to launch the new onboarding experience while continuing to monitor key guardrail metrics.

# Hypothesis Test Interpretation

### Null Hypothesis (H₀)

There is no difference in Paid Conversion Rate between the Control and Treatment groups.

### Alternative Hypothesis (H₁)

The Treatment group has a higher Paid Conversion Rate than the Control group.

### Test Conducted

Two-Proportion Z-Test

### Significance Level

α = 0.05

### Result

The p-value obtained from the analysis was below 0.05.

### Conclusion

The Null Hypothesis was rejected.

This indicates that the Treatment onboarding experience generated a statistically significant improvement in Paid Conversion Rate and that the improvement is unlikely to be due to chance.
